About this tool

SUHU is an interpretation layer, not an independent forecast. It does not run its own ocean model. It aggregates the world's ENSO / IOD / MJO monitoring — including NOAA's — and translates it into what those drivers mean for rainfall, drought and haze in Malaysia. For the underlying science, NOAA, BoM, JAMSTEC and JMA remain the authorities.

How this differs from NOAA ▾
  • Multi-driver, not ENSO-only. NOAA CPC centres on the Pacific (Niño 3.4, ONI). SUHU combines ENSO + the Indian Ocean Dipole + the MJO + the monsoon calendar — the drivers that actually govern Malaysian rainfall.
  • Tuned to the Maritime Continent. NOAA's core indices sit in the eastern Pacific, the far side from us. SUHU weights the sources that face this region — Australia's BoM and Japan's JAMSTEC / JMA.
  • Interprets locally. NOAA reports the state of the Pacific. SUHU translates it into consequences by region (Peninsula west/east, Sabah, Sarawak) and by monsoon season — drought & haze vs flood risk.
  • Multi-source & fault-tolerant. NOAA is one agency. SUHU aggregates several with automatic fallback (e.g. the DMI JAMSTEC→PSL chain), so no single source going down blinds the tool.

In short: NOAA answers "what is the Pacific doing?" — SUHU answers "given what NOAA, BoM and JAMSTEC all say, what does it mean for Malaysia this season?"
